  1. Power Connector: This is the main connector that supplies power to the motherboard. It is usually a 24-pin ATX power connector.
  2. CPU Power Connector: This connector provides power to the CPU. It is typically an 8-pin or 4-pin ATX power connector
  3. SATA Connector: This is used to connect hard drives, SSDs, and optical drives to the motherboard. It is a small, rectangular connector.
  4. PCIe Slots: These are used for expansion cards such as graphics cards, sound cards, and network cards. They are longer than other connectors and usually have a locking mechanism to secure the card in place.
  5. USB Ports: These are used for connecting external devices such as keyboards, mice, and printers. They are typically rectangular in shape and come in different versions such as USB 2.0, USB 3.0, and USB-C.
  6. Audio Jacks: These are used for connecting speakers or headphones to the motherboard. There are usually three jacks for stereo audio and one jack for a microphone.
  7. Ethernet Port: This is used for connecting to a wired network. It is a rectangular port with a clip to hold the cable in place.
