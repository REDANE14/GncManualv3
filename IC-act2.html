  • COMPUTE THE WATTAGE USAGE

Instruction

Tips! Not all computer components are using the same wattage. Make sure to note all pc components this is depend on the brand and the unit. Goodluck!

  1. Identify the components that draw power: The power usage of a computer is determined by the power draw of its components, such as the CPU, GPU, motherboard, and hard drive.
  2. Determine the power draw of each component: You can find the power consumption of each component by checking its documentation or by looking up its specifications online. The power consumption is usually measured in watts (W).
  3. Calculate the total power usage: Once you have the power consumption of each component, you can add them up to determine the total power usage. For example, if your CPU draws 65W, your GPU draws 150W, your hard drive draws 10W, and your monitor draws 30W, your total power usage would be 255W.
  4. Consider any additional factors: Keep in mind that the power usage of a computer can also be affected by factors like the power supply efficiency, the workload on the components, and any overclocking or undervolting. You may also want to factor in the power usage of any other devices that are plugged into the same outlet, as this can affect the overall power consumption of your home or office.
